Our problem is that teens and young adults are addicted to screens and technology.

Devices offer a constant stream of notifications, updates, and entertainment that can be difficult to ignore. ZeroScreen helps uers avoid these in helathy ways.

Many teens do not even realize that they are addicted to screens, as social media has become an integral part of many teens' social lives. Addressing this problem would be highly beneficial to teens currently struggling with screen addiction since it would reduce the anxiety and stress induced by social media and screens. The primary demographic of people that are affected by screen addiction are teenagers, as their brains are more susceptible to becoming addicted, leading to issues in development (Azaret, 2024). Ultimately, screen addiction is a major issue in teens because it takes up much of their time and causes unfavorable mental health issues.

Our story

Our team is interested in this problem because like most teens, we spend a lot of time consuming digital content and playing mobile games. We ourselves know and may sometimes feel addicted to screens and we want to find out what causes the addiction. Furthermore, we would like to research some ways to reduce screen usage and find other activities to substitute for screen usage. Our team members have social media and play video games, and we often lose track of how long we have been on screens. At first, it may feel fun as an escape from reality, but the moment we turn off the screen, all that time feels wasted and we feel regret. Some of us have and might even in the future regret not doing something more memorable, fun and better for ourselves. The more time we spend on our screens, the less time we will have to study, socialize, and make meaningful contributions to our future.
